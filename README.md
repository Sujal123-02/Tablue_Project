# Toy Retail Sales & Branch Performance Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive **Time Series & Branch Performance Analysis Dashboard** for a multi-branch toy retail company operating across multiple countries.

The dashboard analyzes toy sales data from **2022–2025** to identify:

- Peak toy purchase months
- Weekly sales distribution patterns
- Branch-wise growth rates
- Country-level revenue contribution
- Forecasted revenue trends
- Top-performing branch

The goal is to support data-driven retail strategy, seasonal inventory planning, and branch performance optimization.

---

# 📊 Dashboard 1: Time Series & Sales Trends

## 1️⃣ Peak Purchase Months (Branch-wise)

- Monthly toy sales comparison across branches
- Identification of seasonal demand spikes
- Multi-year trend evaluation

🧠 **Key Insight:**  
Toy sales show strong seasonal peaks, likely aligned with holiday seasons, festive periods, and promotional campaigns.

---

## 2️⃣ Weekly Sales Distribution

- Week-by-week sales breakdown
- Identification of high-activity sales weeks
- Volatility and purchasing concentration analysis

📌 **Observation:**  
Sales occur in sharp spikes rather than steady flow, suggesting event-driven or festive buying behavior.

---

## 3️⃣ Branch-wise Growth Rate (Yearly, Quarterly, Monthly)

- Treemap growth visualization
- Positive and negative growth detection
- Performance benchmarking across branches

📈 Some branches show consistent quarterly growth, while others experience fluctuating seasonal demand.

---

## 4️⃣ Forecasted Revenue

- Historical revenue trend (Actual)
- Projected revenue (Forecast)
- Estimated continuation into 2026

🔮 Forecast suggests stable revenue with moderate growth, assuming consistent seasonal demand patterns.

# 📊 Key Business Insights

- Toy sales are highly seasonal.
- Revenue spikes align with probable festive/holiday demand cycles.
- Branch D consistently outperforms others.
- Weekly sales show campaign-driven purchasing patterns.
- Geographic segmentation plays a significant role in revenue variation.
